
Horses are how you travel between locations. Without a horse you're walking — slow, vulnerable, exposed. Money is tight early, so you can steal horses or find them free range instead of buying.

### Modifiers

Modifiers describe a horse's condition or training. A horse can have multiple at once.

|Modifier|Meaning|Effect|
|---|---|---|
|Green|Young, untrained|Gains stats through rides. Unpredictable early.|
|Reined|Well trained to rein pressure|Passive bonus — better handling in chases|
|Lathered|Pushed too hard, covered in sweat foam|Needs rest immediately or risks injury|
|Spooked|Frightened|Harder to control, may bolt|

### Rankings

#### Starting Mount

**Mule** — Not even a horse. Slow, stubborn, barely serviceable. Gets you there eventually.

#### Tier 1 — Early Game

1. **Green Bronco** — Cheap and dangerous. High ceiling, low floor. Could be great or get you killed.
2. **Green Mare** — Affordable early upgrade. Needs time to develop.

#### Tier 2 — Mid Game

3. **Pinto Gelding** — Solid and reliable. Forgettable but gets the job done.
4. **Roan Mare** — Dependable endurance horse. Great for long rides and escapes.
5. **Mustang** — Wild card. Tough and cheap if you can find one but unpredictable.
6. **Buckskin Gelding** — Underrated workhorse. Best stamina in the mid tier. The smart buy.

#### Tier 3 — Late Game

7. **Quarter Horse** — The standard top tier. Fast, reliable, versatile. Every outlaw wants one.
8. **Palomino Stallion** — Fast and powerful but raises heat and hard to manage. High risk high reward.
9. **Reined Roan Stallion** — Rare, expensive, elite. Built for speed and endurance. The horse a legend rides.
